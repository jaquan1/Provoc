Hilary Ronen

District 9 seat for the San Francisco Board of Supervisors

Bio

Hillary Ronen, 40, a resident of the Portola, is an attorney and neighborhood advocate with over 14 years of experience working on behalf of District 9 residents. A graduate of from Boalt Hall School of Law at UC Berkeley, she worked as a civil rights attorney in the Mission and Bernal for 6 years before becoming Chief-of-Staff to Supervisor David Campos. Hillary is married to Francisco Ugarte, Immigration Specialist at the San Francisco Public Defender's office. Their daughter Maelle recently celebrated her third birthday. If elected to office, Hillary will be the first mother to serve as a San Francisco Supervisor in over 5 years.

Platform

For 14 years Hillary has been fighting to make sure that District 9 gets its fair share in City Hall. She’s been a strong negotiator for our neighborhoods; winning affordable housing in The Mission, funding for crime prevention in Bernal Heights, and parks and green spaces for the Portola. As the Chief-of-Staff for the District 9 Supervisor’s Office, Hillary is known for bringing people together from across the aisle to pass legislation that’s helped families during the Affordability Crisis. Hillary helped author and pass Free Muni for Youth, strong eviction protections for families and teachers, new policies that address the growing homeless encampments in our neighborhoods, and the first legislation in the country to require equal pay for women. Issues that she is focused on:

  • Build 5,000 units of affordable housing in the next 10 years by leveraging state and federal funding, and purchasing land for the City to build housing on.
  • Protect Legacy Businesses in our neighborhoods from unfair rent hikes and evictions. Create a universal preschool program by following the example of New York and other cities around the world, by adding public preschool to our K-12 systems.
  • Address homeless encampments by building additional Navigation Centers that connect homeless people with mental health services, bathrooms, showers, and housing.
  • Create a special property crime unit that uses programs like bait cars, hot spot cameras, and crime reporting apps to solve property crimes and car break-ins.
  • Make streets safe for pedestrians by creating safe walkways, crosswalks, and open spaces.
  • Create a trilingual school system that helps all San Francisco schoolchildren become proficient in our city’s three major languages: Spanish, Chinese, and English.
  • Give all families healthy food options and address our many food deserts, by finding public spaces for farmers’ markets and nonprofit groceries that are accessible to all neighborhoods.
